### Abstract

The rapid demand for high-efficiency and environmentally benign photovoltaic absorbers has intensified the search for lead-free halide perovskites that can simultaneously deliver strong optoelectronic response and long-term stability. In this context, double perovskites offer an attractive design space due to their compositional flexibility, suppressed Pb-toxicity concerns, and improved structural robustness compared to many Pb-based counterparts. Motivated by these considerations, we investigate the cubic lead-free double perovskite Cs2NaFeBr6 using density functional theory and complementary stability and device-metric analyses. Geometric formability indicators confirm the feasibility of the cubic framework with t = 0.9724, µ = 0.425, and τ = 0.425, while thermodynamic evaluation yields a negative formation energy and a positive decomposition energy, supporting energetic stability. Phonon dispersion shows no imaginary frequencies, establishing dynamical stability. The mechanical properties confirm a mechanically stable, relatively soft halide lattice, and the elastic constants further suggest ductile behavior. The Debye temperature is estimated to be ΘD ≈ 254 K, consistent with the comparatively soft bonding environment of halide frameworks. Electronic-structure calculations reveal a direct bandgap of 1.09 eV, which lies close to the optimum window for single-junction photovoltaics. Carrier-transport descriptors derived from band-edge curvature show moderate effective masses implying favorable electron transport and reasonably balanced carrier dynamics. Optical response exhibits strong dielectric screening with a static dielectric constant ε1(0) ≈ 6.5, supporting reduced Coulombic trapping and recombination. Finally, spectroscopic limited maximum efficiency analysis under AM 1.5G illumination predicts high photovoltaic performance, yielding a thickness-dependent power conversion efficiency (PCE) of 30.60% at 1 µm and a maximum PCE of 32.09% at 5 µm, approaching the Shockley-Queisser ceiling for this bandgap. Collectively, the stability signatures and optoelectronic metrics position Cs2NaFeBr6 as a promising lead-free absorber for high-efficiency photovoltaic devices and motivate further experimental validation.
